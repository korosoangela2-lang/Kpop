K-pop: A Global Music Phenomenon

K-pop (Korean Pop) originated in South Korea in the 1990s, blending Western genres with Korean culture.

Groups like BTS, BLACKPINK, and EXO dominate globally with polished performances and massive fanbases.

Popular K-pop Groups

  • BTS: Known for meaningful lyrics and powerful performances.
  • BLACKPINK: Fierce style and catchy songs, global icons.
  • EXO: Strong vocals and dynamic performances.

K-pop Fans

K-pop fans, or "stans," are passionate supporters who organize events, stream music, and promote their idols worldwide.

About BTS

BTS debuted in 2013 under BigHit Entertainment. They are known for meaningful lyrics, powerful performances, and global influence.

Achievements

  • First K-pop group to top Billboard Hot 100.
  • Grammy nominations and UN speeches.
  • Global fanbase known as ARMY.

About EXO

EXO debuted in 2012 under SM Entertainment. They are known for strong vocals, synchronized choreography, and diverse music styles.

  • Major influence in Asia and beyond.
  • Hit songs like "Growl" and "Love Shot".

BLACKPINK debuted in 2016 under YG Entertainment. They are known for fierce style, catchy songs, and global popularity.
